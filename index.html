Dr. A.P.J ABDUL KALAM

Former Indian President - Missile Man Of India

15 October 1931 - 27 July 2015

"If you want to shine like a sun then first burn like a sun"

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, affectionately known as the "Missile Man of India," was a visionary leader and an inspiration to countless individuals, including myself, as a graduate student. His contributions to India's space research and development have left an indelible mark on our nation's progress. Dr. Kalam played a pivotal role in the successful development and testing of critical missile technologies, catapulting India into the league of space-faring nations. Beyond his scientific achievements, Dr. Kalam possessed a unique ability to connect with and inspire the youth of our country. His unwavering belief in the potential of young minds to shape the future was evident in his countless interactions with students and his emphasis on education and innovation. He encouraged us to dream big, work tirelessly, and strive for excellence in our chosen fields. Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam's legacy as a scientist, leader, and motivator continues to guide us as we embark on our academic journeys. His vision for a prosperous and technologically advanced India, powered by the aspirations of its youth, remains a beacon of hope and inspiration for all of us, as we strive to contribute to our nation's progress.
